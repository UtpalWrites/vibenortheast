Explore The Destinations

Kamakhya Temple

One of the most powerful Shakti Peethas situated atop Nilachal Hill. Famous for the Ambubachi Mela.

Umananda Island

The smallest inhabited river island in the world, located in the middle of the Brahmaputra River.

Panimur Waterfalls

Known as the 'Niagara of Assam,' this breathtaking waterfall on the Kopili River offers a mesmerizing view.

Charaideo Maidam

The sacred burial grounds of the Ahom Kings, often compared to the Pyramids of Egypt. A UNESCO site.

Majuli

The world's largest river island and the cultural capital of Assam. Famous for its Vaishnavite Satras.

Manas National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its Project Tiger reserve and wild water buffaloes.

Tea Gardens

Walk through the lush green carpet of Assam's famous tea estates and stay in heritage bungalows.

Rang Ghar

Asia's oldest amphitheater, used by Ahom royals. A magnificent two-story red brick structure in Sivasagar.

Seven Sisters Falls

A spectacular seven-segmented waterfall located near Cherrapunjee.

Mawsmai Cave

A thrilling limestone cave experience with narrow passages and unique stalactite formations.

Laitlum Canyon

Meaning 'End of Hills,' this canyon offers breathtaking panoramic views of deep gorges.

Shillong

Known as the 'Scotland of the East' for its rolling hills and colonial charm.

Dawki River

Famous for the Umngot River with its crystal clear waters where boats appear to float on air.

Mawlynnong

Acclaimed as Asia's cleanest village. Visitors can enjoy the Sky Walk and local culture.

Living Root Bridge

A marvel of bio-engineering created by Khasi tribes using rubber tree roots.

Nohkalikai Falls

The tallest plunge waterfall in India at 340 meters, featuring a stunning blue pool.

One Horned Rhino

Kaziranga hosts two-thirds of the world's Great One-Horned Rhinoceros population.

Spotted Deer

The park is home to large herds of Eastern Swamp Deer and Hog Deer.

Wild Water Buffalo

Kaziranga has the largest population of Wild Water Buffalo globally.

Kaziranga Orchids

Visit the Orchid and Biodiversity Park to see over 600 varieties of wild orchids.

Jeep Safari

Experience the thrill of the jungle with an open jeep safari to spot wildlife up close.

Madhuri Lake

This high-altitude lake was formed by an earthquake and offers stunning alpine views.

Mechuka Valley

Often called the 'Mini Switzerland' of Arunachal with snow-capped peaks and pine forests.

Bumla Pass

A historic mountain pass at the Indo-China border at 15,200 ft.

Tawang Monastery

India's largest and the world's second-largest monastery, perched at 10,000 ft.

Sela Pass

A high-altitude mountain pass that serves as the stunning gateway to Tawang.

Ziro Valley

A lush green valley home to the Apatani tribe, famous for their unique culture.

Dzukou Valley

Known as the 'Valley of Flowers of the Northeast.' A trekker's paradise.

Japfu Peak

The second highest peak in Nagaland, offering stunning views of Dzukou Valley.

Khonoma Village

Asia's first Green Village, famous for its history and modern conservation efforts.

Hornbill Festival

The 'Festival of Festivals' showcasing the rich culture of all Naga tribes.

Kohima

The historic capital city, famous for the WWII War Cemetery.

Shree Govindajee

A historic Vaishnavite temple located next to the royal palace in Imphal.

Sadu Chiru Falls

A picturesque spot near Imphal requiring a short hike through lush greenery.

Keibul Lamjao

The world's only floating National Park and home of the endangered Sangai.

Loktak Lake

The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, famous for floating swamps.

Kangla Fort

The ancient seat of the Meitei rulers in the heart of Imphal.

Aizawl

The scenic capital city perched on vertical ridges overlooking the valley.

Reiek Tlang

A mystic mountain peak offering breathtaking panoramic views.

Dampa Tiger Reserve

A dense jungle sanctuary perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Phawngpui Peak

Known as the Blue Mountain, famous for its orchids and stunning cliff views.

Vantawng Falls

The highest and most spectacular waterfall in Mizoram.
