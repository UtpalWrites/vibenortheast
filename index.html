Photo Gallery

Kamakhya Temple

One of the most powerful Shakti Peethas situated atop Nilachal Hill. Famous for the Ambubachi Mela and its unique Tantric traditions.

Umananda Island

The smallest inhabited river island in the world, located in the middle of the Brahmaputra River. It houses a historic Shiva temple.

Panimur Waterfalls

Known as the 'Niagara of Assam,' this breathtaking waterfall on the Kopili River offers a mesmerizing panoramic view and a perfect picnic spot.

Charaideo Maidam

The sacred burial grounds of the Ahom Kings, often compared to the Pyramids of Egypt. A UNESCO World Heritage site representing Ahom glory.

Majuli

The world's largest river island and the cultural capital of Assam. Famous for its Vaishnavite Satras (monasteries) and mask-making culture.

Manas National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its Project Tiger reserve, wild water buffaloes, and rare golden langurs set against the Bhutan Himalayas.

Tea Gardens

Walk through the lush green carpet of Assam's famous tea estates. Experience the aroma of fresh tea leaves and stay in heritage bungalows.

Rang Ghar

Asia's oldest amphitheater, used by Ahom royals for sports like buffalo fighting. A magnificent two-story red brick structure in Sivasagar.

Seven Sisters Falls

A spectacular seven-segmented waterfall located near Cherrapunjee. It is one of the highest waterfalls in India, best viewed during the monsoon.

Mawsmai Cave

A thrilling limestone cave experience with narrow passages and unique stalactite formations. It is fully lit and accessible for tourists.

Laitlum Canyon

Meaning 'End of Hills,' this canyon offers breathtaking panoramic views of deep gorges and winding rivers. A perfect spot for nature lovers.

Shillong

Known as the 'Scotland of the East' for its rolling hills and colonial charm. Famous for Ward's Lake, Police Bazar, and vibrant music culture.

Dawki River

Famous for the Umngot River with its crystal clear waters where boats appear to float on air. A border town near Bangladesh offering camping.

Mawlynnong

Acclaimed as Asia's cleanest village. Visitors can enjoy the Sky Walk, local cleanliness initiatives, and living root bridges nearby.

Living Root Bridge

A marvel of bio-engineering created by Khasi tribes using rubber tree roots. The Double Decker bridge in Nongriat is a world-famous trekking spot.

Nohkalikai Falls

The tallest plunge waterfall in India at 340 meters. It features a stunning blue pool at the bottom and has a tragic local legend attached to it.

One Horned Rhino

The pride of Assam. Kaziranga hosts two-thirds of the world's Great One-Horned Rhinoceros population, making it a top wildlife destination.

Spotted Deer

The park is home to large herds of Eastern Swamp Deer (Barasingha) and Hog Deer, which are vital to the park's healthy tiger ecosystem.

Wild Water Buffalo

Kaziranga has the largest population of Wild Water Buffalo globally. These massive beasts with huge horns are a common sight in the wetlands.

Kaziranga Orchids

Visit the Orchid and Biodiversity Park to see over 600 varieties of wild orchids, bamboo gardens, and enjoy cultural dance performances.

Jeep Safari

Experience the thrill of the jungle with an open jeep safari. Explore the four ranges of the park to spot Rhinos, Elephants, and Tigers up close.

Madhuri Lake

Originally Sangestar Tso, this high-altitude lake was formed by an earthquake. It became famous after the Bollywood movie 'Koyla' was filmed here.

Mechuka Valley

Often called the 'Mini Switzerland' of Arunachal. A picturesque valley near the China border with snow-capped peaks, pine forests, and the Siyom River.

Bumla Pass

A historic mountain pass at the Indo-China border at 15,200 ft. Visitors can see the border personnel meeting point with special Army permission.

Tawang Monastery

India's largest and the world's second-largest monastery. Perched at 10,000 ft, it is the spiritual center of the Monpa people.

Sela Pass

A high-altitude mountain pass at 13,700 ft that serves as the gateway to Tawang. It features the stunning frozen Sela Lake.

Ziro Valley

A lush green valley home to the Apatani tribe, famous for their unique facial tattoos, paddy-cum-fish cultivation, and the Ziro Music Festival.

Dzukou Valley

Known as the 'Valley of Flowers of the Northeast.' A trekker's paradise famous for its rolling hills, seasonal flowers, and unique bamboo brush.

Japfu Peak

The second highest peak in Nagaland, offering stunning views of Dzukou Valley. Famous for the world's tallest rhododendron tree found here.

Khonoma Village

Asia's first Green Village. This Angami settlement is famous for its history of resisting British rule and its modern conservation efforts.

Hornbill Festival

The 'Festival of Festivals' held every December. It showcases the rich culture, dance, music, and food of all Naga tribes in one place.

Kohima

The historic capital city, famous for the WWII War Cemetery dedicated to soldiers who died halting the Japanese invasion of India.

Shree Govindajee

A historic Vaishnavite temple located next to the royal palace in Imphal. Famous for its golden twin domes and grand Holi celebrations.

Sadu Chiru Falls

Also known as Leimaram Waterfall, this picturesque spot near Imphal requires a short hike through lush greenery to reach the cascading water.

Keibul Lamjao

The world's only floating National Park located on Loktak Lake. It is the last natural habitat of the endangered Sangai (dancing deer).

Loktak Lake

The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, famous for its circular floating swamps called 'Phumdis' and vibrant boating tourism.

Kangla Fort

The ancient seat of the Meitei rulers. A sprawling complex in the heart of Imphal filled with sacred shrines, moats, and historical ruins.

Aizawl

The scenic capital city perched on vertical ridges overlooking the valley. Famous for its pleasant weather, Solomon's Temple, and vibrant culture.

Reiek Tlang

A mystic mountain peak near Aizawl offering breathtaking panoramic views. It is also home to a model Mizo heritage village.

Dampa Tiger Reserve

The largest wildlife sanctuary in Mizoram. A dense jungle home to tigers, leopards, and clouded leopards, perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Phawngpui Peak

Known as the Blue Mountain, it is the highest peak in Mizoram. Famous for its orchids, rhododendrons, and stunning cliff views.

Vantawng Falls

The highest and most spectacular waterfall in Mizoram, located in the Serchhip district. It cascades down from a height of 750 feet.
